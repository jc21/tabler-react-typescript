# Dropdown

Use dropdowns to display lists of options or include more positions in a menu
without overwhelming users with too many buttons and long lists. Dropdowns
facilitate users' interaction with your website or software and make your design
look clear.

## Usage

<Playground>
	<Dropdown className="demo">
		<Dropdown.Item>Action</Dropdown.Item>
		<Dropdown.Item>Another action</Dropdown.Item>
		<Dropdown.Item divider />
		<Dropdown.Item>Third action</Dropdown.Item>
	</Dropdown>
</Playground>

## Active

Make a dropdown item look active, so that it highlights when a user hovers over
a given option.

<Playground>
	<Dropdown className="demo">
		<Dropdown.Item>Action</Dropdown.Item>
		<Dropdown.Item active>Another action</Dropdown.Item>
	</Dropdown>
</Playground>

## Disabled

Make a dropdown item look disabled to display options which are currently not
available but can activate once certain conditions are met.

<Playground>
	<Dropdown className="demo">
		<Dropdown.Item>Action</Dropdown.Item>
		<Dropdown.Item disabled>Another action</Dropdown.Item>
	</Dropdown>
</Playground>

## Header

Add a dropdown header to group dropdown items into sections and name them
accordingly.

<Playground>
	<Dropdown className="demo" header="Dropdown Header">
		<Dropdown.Item>Action</Dropdown.Item>
		<Dropdown.Item>Another action</Dropdown.Item>
	</Dropdown>
</Playground>

## Icons

Use icons in your dropdowns to add more visual content and make the options easy
to identify for users.

<Playground>
	<Dropdown className="demo">
		<Dropdown.Item icon={<Edit />}>Action</Dropdown.Item>
		<Dropdown.Item icon={<Settings />}>Another action</Dropdown.Item>
	</Dropdown>
</Playground>

## Arrow

Add an arrow that points at the dropdown button.

<Playground>
	<Dropdown className="demo" arrow>
		<Dropdown.Item icon={<Edit />}>Action</Dropdown.Item>
		<Dropdown.Item icon={<Settings />}>Another action</Dropdown.Item>
	</Dropdown>
</Playground>

## Badges

Add a badge to your dropdown items to show additional information related to an
item or distinguish it from other elements.

<Playground>
	<Dropdown className="demo">
		<Dropdown.Item icon={<Edit />}>
			Action
			<Badge className="ms-auto" color="primary">
				12
			</Badge>
		</Dropdown.Item>
		<Dropdown.Item icon={<Settings />}>
			Another action <Badge className="ms-auto" color="success" />
		</Dropdown.Item>
	</Dropdown>
</Playground>

## Dark

Make your dropdown suit the dark mode of your website or software.

<Playground>
	<Dropdown className="demo" header="Dropdown Header" arrow dark>
		<Dropdown.Item icon={<Edit />}>
			Action{" "}
			<Badge className="ms-auto" color="warning">
				12
			</Badge>
		</Dropdown.Item>
		<Dropdown.Item icon={<Settings />}>Another action</Dropdown.Item>
		<Dropdown.Item divider />
		<Dropdown.Item active>Third action</Dropdown.Item>
		<Dropdown.Item disabled>Fourth action</Dropdown.Item>
	</Dropdown>
</Playground>
